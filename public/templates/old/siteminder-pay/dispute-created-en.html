Next steps

Common reasons for a payment dispute include guests not recognising the business name on their statement, or guests being unaware of a deposit being charged.
Contact the cardholder

As a first step we recommend contacting the cardholder to understand why they raised the dispute. If it was made in error you can ask the cardholder to withdraw.
Challenge the dispute

If the payment was valid but the guest refuses to withdraw the dispute, or it was made in bad faith, you can challenge the dispute with the card issuer.
To begin the process, reply to this e-mail and include any evidence supporting your right to keep the disputed payment. We will submit the evidence to the card issuer on your behalf.
Types of evidence

  • Correspondence such as text messages and e-mails between you and the cardholder
  • Evidence of the supply of goods and services
  • Identification collected at check-in

Your evidence is required by {{dueDate}}.

Once evidence has been submitted, card issuers generally take 60 to 75 days to reach a final decision. If the dispute is resolved in your favour any money that may have been taken will be paid back to you.

If you do not submit any evidence, it’s unlikely the dispute will be resolved in your favour.
